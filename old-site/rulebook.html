QUEST ESPORTS – VALORANT TOURNAMENT RULEBOOK

This page contains the official rules for Quest Esports VALORANT tournaments in Sri Lanka. General Rules apply to all tournaments, while Women’s Tournament Rules apply only to female-only events and override the general rules where specified.

1. Introduction

Quest Esports reserves the right to amend rules, schedules, or formats whenever necessary in order to preserve fair competition, tournament integrity, and player safety.

2. General Rules (All Tournaments)

2.1 Player & Team Eligibility

  • All players must register and compete using their own valid Riot ID.
  • Using another player’s account, fake information, or false identity is strictly prohibited.
  • Quest Esports may request identity verification using NIC, passport, student ID, or equivalent documents.
  • Failure or refusal to provide verification may result in immediate disqualification.
  • Team rosters become locked once the team has played its first official match.
  • No roster changes are allowed after the first official match unless Quest Esports approves an exceptional case.
  • Team names, player names, logos, and profile images must not contain offensive, obscene, or discriminatory content.

2.2 Tournament Format

  • Tournament format will be announced before each event.
  • Teams consist of 5 core players with up to 2 substitutes.
  • Substitutions are allowed only between maps, never during a round.
  • Stand-ins who are not officially registered are not allowed in online events.

2.3 Match Scheduling

  • Online tournaments: teams must check in 30 minutes before the match start time.
  • LAN tournaments: teams must check in 1 hour before the match start time.
  • A team not ready within 10 minutes of the official start time may forfeit the match.
  • Matches may be played 4v5 only if the opponent agrees.

2.4 Lobby & Game Settings

  • Mode: Tournament
  • Default Server: Singapore
  • Cheats: Off
  • Overtime: Win by Two
  • Lobby Type: Closed
  • Only tournament staff and official casters may observe.

2.4.1 Server Change Exception

  • A different server may be used only if both teams agree.
  • Any server change must be approved by tournament admins before the match begins.
  • Once the match starts, the server cannot be changed.
  • If both teams do not agree, the Singapore server will be used.

2.5 Coaches

  • Each team may have one coach.
  • Coaches may communicate only during map/agent select, tactical timeouts, halftime, and between maps.
  • Teams may use Discord, TeamSpeak, or similar communication platforms.
  • If TeamSpeak is used, admins must be informed in advance.

2.6 Gameplay Rules

  • Hacking, scripting, macros, exploits, and third-party cheats are strictly prohibited.
  • Smurfing, account sharing, or impersonation results in instant disqualification.
  • Match-fixing, collusion, or bribery may result in a permanent ban.
  • Toxicity, harassment, or discrimination may result in immediate disqualification.
  • Tournaments follow Riot Games’ official seasonal map pool.
  • New agents are locked for 2 weeks after release.
  • New maps are locked for 4 weeks after release.

2.7 Pauses

  • Each team gets 2 tactical timeouts per map, 60 seconds each.
  • One additional timeout is allowed in overtime.
  • Technical pauses are allowed for up to 10 minutes per map.
  • The reason for the technical pause must be clearly stated.

2.8 Crashes & Disconnects

  • If a crash happens before damage is dealt, the round may be replayed.
  • If significant gameplay has already happened, the round continues.
  • An entire map may be replayed only at admin discretion.

2.9 Organizer Rights

  • Quest Esports may adjust schedules, formats, or match outcomes when necessary to ensure fairness.
  • Prize distribution timelines and requirements will be announced for each event separately.

2.10 Prize Distribution

  • Prize money will be sent to the team captain within 7 working days.
  • The team captain must provide bank details within 3 days after the tournament ends.

2.11 Map Veto System

BO1
  • Team A bans 1 map
  • Team B bans 1 map
  • Team A bans 1 map
  • Team B bans 1 map
  • Team A bans 1 map
  • Team B bans 1 map
  • Map 7 remains and Team A chooses side
BO3
  • Team A bans 1 map
  • Team B bans 1 map
  • Team A picks Map 1 and Team B chooses side
  • Team B picks Map 2 and Team A chooses side
  • Team A bans 1 map
  • Team B bans 1 map
  • Map 3 remains and Team A chooses side
BO5
  • Team A bans 1 map
  • Team B bans 1 map
  • Team A picks Map 1 and Team B chooses side
  • Team B picks Map 2 and Team A chooses side
  • Team A picks Map 3 and Team B chooses side
  • Team B picks Map 4 and Team A chooses side
  • Map 5 remains and Team B chooses side

2.12 Additional Eligibility Rules

2.12.1 Regional Restriction
  • All Quest Esports VALORANT tournaments are currently restricted to Sri Lankan residents only.
  • Players must be living in Sri Lanka at the time of the tournament.
  • Proof of residency may be requested if false information is suspected.
2.12.2 Community Tournament Participation
  • Coaches, organizers, staff members, and admins may participate as players.
  • They cannot referee or administer their own matches.
  • Another authorized tournament official must handle rulings for those matches.
2.12.3 Player Account Update Procedure

To update or correct player details, the Team Captain must contact Quest Esports using the official channels below:

  • Email: questtesports@gmail.com
  • WhatsApp: 076 119 5666 (Ms. Senumi)
  • WhatsApp: 076 718 6060 (Mr. Russel)
  • An additional referee number may be given if needed.

Each request must include:

  • Tournament Name
  • Team Name
  • Player Name
  • Riot ID
  • Discord Username
  • Corrections and roster updates are allowed only before the first official match.
  • No changes are accepted once a team has started a match.
  • Requests from non-captain or unregistered email addresses will not be accepted.
2.12.4 Roster Lock & Player Changes
  • Players may be changed or substitutes added only before the first official match.
  • Once a team has played its first official match, the roster is fully locked.
  • No further player additions or replacements are permitted after that point.

2.13 Spectra Client Requirement

  • All players must have the Spectra Client installed and running.
  • This is mandatory for in-game data tracking and stream overlays.
  • Players must run the Spectra Player Client as Administrator.
  • Spectra must be started before launching VALORANT.
  • Players must select EU as the server inside Spectra.
  • Failure to comply may result in delays, penalties, or match forfeiture.

2.14 Discord Presence, Spectra & Webcam Requirement

  • If a team uses a coach, that coach must be present on Discord for all matches.
  • When a coach is present, the whole team must stay in the same Discord voice channel with the coach throughout the match.
  • If the team has no coach, the Team Captain or Team Leader must remain on Discord during the match.
  • If webcams are required by tournament admins, all players must keep webcams on for the entire match.
  • Webcam feeds may be used for live broadcasts and competitive integrity monitoring.
Webcam Setup & Verification
  • Admins will provide a Spectra verification link before the match.
  • Players must open the link in a web browser.
  • Players must enter the same Riot ID used for tournament registration.
  • The webcam feed must stay active and uninterrupted during the match.
  • The Spectra browser page must stay open throughout the match.
Phone Webcam Usage
  • Players using a mobile phone as a webcam must disable screen lock or auto-rotate lock.
  • The phone must be rotated into landscape mode.
  • The webcam view must remain stable and correctly oriented.
Webcam Angles
  • Players must follow the approved webcam angles given by tournament administrators.
  • Detailed examples will be shared before the match.
Compliance & Penalties
  • Disabling the webcam, closing Spectra, using unapproved angles, or entering incorrect Riot ID details may result in warnings, penalties, forfeits, or disqualification.
  • Additional Spectra or webcam instructions may be given by admins when required.

3. Women’s Tournament – Special Rules

3.1 Eligibility

  • Players must identify only as female (cisgender).
  • All players must be Sri Lankan citizens or residents.
  • Minimum age is 10 years, and parental consent is required for players under 16.
  • Riot ID must match the registration details.
  • Quest Esports may request official ID for verification.

3.2 Team Formation

  • Teams must have 5 core players.
  • Up to 2 substitutes are allowed.
  • Solo registrations may be placed into teams by the organizers.

3.3 Registration & Check-In

  • Registration deadlines will be announced for each event.
  • Teams must check in 30 minutes before the match start time.
  • Late arrival without notice may result in a forfeit.

3.4 Match Format

  • All matches are 5v5 online.
  • Preliminary matches may be BO1 or BO3 depending on the number of teams.
  • Finals may be BO3 or BO5.
  • The elimination format will be announced before the event.

3.5 CAM-on Refereeing

  • Video verification through Zoom, Discord, or Google Meet may be mandatory when requested.
  • Teams must inform organizers of the chosen platform 30 minutes before the match.
  • Refusal or failure to comply may result in disqualification.

3.6 Conduct & Fair Play

  • Toxicity, harassment, or discrimination may result in immediate disqualification.
  • Cheating, smurfing, and account sharing are strictly prohibited.

3.7 Technical & Timeout Rules

  • Each team is allowed 1 technical pause per map for a maximum of 10 minutes.
  • Each team is allowed 1 tactical timeout per side per map, plus 1 in overtime.
